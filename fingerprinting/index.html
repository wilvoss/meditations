What is Digital Fingerprinting?

A digital fingerprint is a unique identifier created by collecting information about your device and browser.

Websites can save a fingerprint to recognize your device when you return. If a site keeps this identifier, it can detect repeat visits from the same browser. When combined with other data, such as cookies or login information, this allows websites to track users over time.

Third-party code from companies like Meta, Google, or Amazon is often included on websites to analyze traffic and support advertising. They can also save and track digital fingerprints, even if cookies are blocked or you are using private browsing.

How can you protect yourself?

In addition to tweaking the most common browsers, consider using browsers that prioritize privacy and have built-in protections against tracking and fingerprinting, such as Brave or DuckDuckGo.

Safari

Safari (v26) automatically injects noise into digital fingerprinting. "Use advanced tracking and fingerprinting protection" is located in the "Advanced" settings tab and has 2 levels of protection. Set yours to "in all browsing" or "All browsing" depending on your device.

Firefox

Firefox (v116) has a setting called "Resist Fingerprinting" that automatically injects noise into digital fingerprinting. It's located in the "Privacy & Security" section of settings under "Enhanced Tracking Protection". Be sure to set yours to "Strict". Learn more here: About Fingerprinting Protection.

Chrome

You can control site permissions (camera, mic, location) and manage extensions: Manage settings and permissions.

You can reduce the number of extensions you use and disable unnecessary plugins to help lower your fingerprint uniqueness: EFF's Top 10 Tips to Protect Your Privacy.
